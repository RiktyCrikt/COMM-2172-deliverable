Applying What I Learned

Focus on expanded education and experiences

Before handing in the JRC: Resume assignment I thought I just had to list down all of my education and experiences and that would be enough. After reading the feedback given to me I realized that it would make for a much stronger resume if I fully flushed out what I did at each of these places. I have included things such as topics covered in class and duties on the job to show the hiring managers exactly what I know and what I can potentially bring to their company.

Strong formatting

A major issue I had when writing cover letter s in the past was figuring out the formatting. In class, something that really resonated with me was that we should reserve as much space as possible to plea our case as to why we think we would be a great for the chosen organization. To do this in my cover letter I decided to keep the address block to one line, and I think I found a format that I like. This gave plenty of space to expand on my work and education history, while also giving it a personal touch.

Another thing that I never thought too much about before was the closing paragraph, which I realize now is just as important as the other elements. That call to action and friendly send off really sets the tone and truly exhibits your professionalism.

Treat job posting requirements as questions.

When I met a career advisor this one of the sticking points for me. I definitely need a substantial amount of practice when it comes down to the interview portion of the job hunt. When I was told to turn requirements into questions it clicked in my head immediately as a way to practice my interview skills. By doing this, I am practicing questions that will most likely be asked during the interview for the specific job I am looking at. This will give me ample time to fully flush out an answer ahead of time, which should in turn make me sound more confident in my answer.

Where do I go from here?

After improving my job ready communication with the help of career services...

...my next step will be to start applying and find the opportunities to set my new interview preparation skills in motion. One of the best tips I got from my interaction was to create a list of 10 companies you want to work for and rank them by how much you want to work for them from most to least.

Once ranked, I will start from the bottom and work my way up the list seeking interviews from each company. This will get ensure that as I move towards the top of the list I will either:

  • Continue to gain interview experience before applying for the job at the top of my list.
  • Get a job in the industry at a lower ranked company.

I believe that this is the next step in my journey because my weakest communication by far was the interview, and I believe that the more I do, the closer I will be to getting that dream role.
